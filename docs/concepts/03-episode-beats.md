# Episode Beats

The week is a show with a five-beat arc. The Stream doesn't just display content chronologically — it **changes character** as the episode progresses. The audience feels the shift without being told.

---

## The Arc

```
MON          TUE-WED          THU          SAT-SUN          MON
 │              │               │              │              │
 ▼              ▼               ▼              ▼              ▼
INTEL        TENSION          THE           THE            THE
DROPS        BUILDS           CALL          MATCH        RECKONING
```

Each beat has a distinct feel: what content appears, how dense the Stream is, what the Crew Bar shows, and what the Interaction Bar offers.

---

## Beat 1: Intel Drops (Monday)

**The feel:** Busy. Fresh. Lots of small things arriving. The show is waking up after the weekend.

### What's in the Stream

- Miner activity cards stacking up fast
  - "4 new episodes overnight"
  - "KingOfSC dropped a Round 7 preview. Flagging for Analyst."
  - "NRLBrothers went deep on Munster's form. 22 minutes on one player."
- The Reckoning cards from the previous round may still be arriving (overlap with previous beat)
- No Remarks yet — the crew is gathering, not calling

### Stream Density

High. Many small cards. Fast-moving. The Stream feels alive after a quiet weekend.

### Crew Bar

```
[●] Miner — Scanning 6 new episodes    [○] Analyst    [○] Critic    [○] Bookkeeper    [●] Archivist — Updating records
```

Miner is the star of this beat. The audience learns: when Miner is busy, intel is coming.

### Interaction Bar

Default state: "Ask Jaromelu something..."

No Remarks to react to yet. The audience is in observation mode.

### Video Moment

None for this beat. Intel Drops is about volume and speed — crew activity cards stacking up. The personality comes from Miner's avatar being visibly busy in the Crew Bar, and the rapid-fire entry animations of activity cards sliding in.

### Transition Out

Not a divider line — a **scene change**. Miner's avatar in the Crew Bar transitions from active to completing (brief nod animation). Analyst's avatar wakes up (eyes opening, colour returning). In the Stream, a transition card appears:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  [👤 Miner fading out →]               │
│       ─── data flowing ───             │
│               [← 👤 Analyst waking up] │
│                                         │
│  TENSION BUILDS                        │
│  Analyst is cross-referencing           │
└─────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

The audience sees the handoff between characters. The scene is changing.

## Beat 2: Tension Builds (Tuesday – Wednesday)

**The feel:** Threading. Contradictions emerging. The picture is forming but unclear. Unresolved questions hang in the air.

### What's in the Stream

- Analyst activity cards with contradiction highlights
  - "3 sources selling Cleary. 1 buying hard. Investigating."
  - "Consensus shift detected: market turned bearish on Cleary since Monday"
  - "Contradiction: KingOfSC and NRLBrothers draw opposite conclusions from the same matchup data"
- Bookkeeper cards with numbers
  - "Cleary breakeven: 42. Needs 55+ to justify. Last 4 weeks trending down."
  - "Cap space after proposed trade: $87k. Tight."
- Archivist cards surfacing history
  - "Last time 3+ sources agreed on a sell: Round 4. Consensus was correct."
- Open threads — visual indicators that something is unresolved
  - A contradiction card might have a "🔓 Unresolved" badge
  - When Analyst resolves it, the badge updates to "✓ Resolved" with the conclusion

### Stream Density

Medium. Cards are longer and more detailed than Miner's Intel Drop cards. The content is heavier — analysis, not discovery.

### Crew Bar

```
[○] Miner    [●] Analyst — Cross-referencing Cleary claims    [○] Critic    [●] Bookkeeper — Running numbers    [●] Archivist — Pulling history
```

Multiple crew members active. The operation is in full swing.

### Interaction Bar

"Ask Jaromelu something..." — audience can ask about developing threads.

### Visual Cues for Tension

- Contradiction cards have a **face-off layout**: two crew member avatars (or expert source images) on opposite sides, with opposing claims and a "vs" divider. A visual confrontation.
- Unresolved threads have a subtle pulsing indicator (something here needs attention)
- Consensus shift cards include a small inline chart showing sentiment movement
- Analyst's avatar in the Crew Bar shows a focused, concentrating expression during this beat

### Video Moment

When a major contradiction is discovered, a short video clip (3-5 seconds) of Analyst's reaction plays inline — raised eyebrow, leaning in to examine something. Optional, reserved for the most significant contradictions.

### Transition Out

Scene change: the crew avatars in the Crew Bar all dim except Jaromelu. His avatar transitions to a focused, decisive expression. In the Stream:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  [👤 Analyst + Bookkeeper fade out →]  │
│                                         │
│         [← 👤 Jaromelu activates]      │
│         [intense, leaning forward]      │
│                                         │
│  THE CALL                               │
│  Jaromelu is reviewing the evidence     │
└─────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

The visual tone shifts. Less activity. More weight. Something is about to happen.

## Beat 3: The Call (Thursday)

**The feel:** Sparse. Bold. The moment of commitment. The Stream goes quiet and one thing dominates the screen. This is the climax of the episode.

### What's in the Stream

- **The Critic confrontation** (if one exists) — this is a **mini-scene**, not just a text card:
  - A face-off layout: Critic's avatar on the left, Jaromelu's on the right
  - The Critic's challenge: "Two of your three sell sources are below 50% accuracy. You sure about this?"
  - Jaromelu's response: "I've watched the tape. I don't care what the accuracy says."
  - Optional: a short **video clip** (5-8 seconds) of the confrontation — Critic with arms crossed, sceptical; Jaromelu unflinching. This is the drama the show promises.
- **Jaromelu's Remark** — big, bold, taking up most of the viewport
  - Full entry animation with video clip for high conviction calls (see [02-remarks.md](02-remarks.md))
  - This is the hero moment. The Remark card is visually maximal.
- Possibly 2-4 Remarks across the afternoon as Jaromelu locks in his calls for the round
- Squad update card: "Squad locked. Trades executed. Captain set. Here's the lineup."

### Stream Density

**Low.** This is the quietest the Stream gets. Lots of whitespace. The Remark dominates. The audience's eye goes to one thing: the call.

If there are multiple Remarks, they arrive spaced out — not stacked. Each one gets its moment.

### Crew Bar

```
[○] Miner    [○] Analyst    [●] Critic — Challenging the Cleary call    [○] Bookkeeper    [○] Archivist    [●] Jaromelu — Making his call
```

Jaromelu and possibly the Critic are the only active crew. Everyone else has done their work.

### Interaction Bar

Contextual — showing reaction buttons for the active Remark:

```
[Agree] [Disagree]  |  Ask about this call...  [→]
```

This is the peak interaction moment. The audience weighs in before the round starts.

### The Moment

This beat should feel like the lights dimming before a performance. The Stream was busy for three days. Now it's quiet. One bold statement. "Here's where I stand. Judge me."

The visual design of this beat matters more than any other. The Remark card during The Call should be the single most polished, screenshottable element in the entire product.

### Video Moment

**This is the peak video beat.** The most important Remark of the round gets a video clip intro. If only one beat in the entire week has video, it's this one.

For high-conviction calls, the video could be a longer clip (8-10 seconds): Jaromelu reviewing evidence, looking conflicted for a moment, then settling into confidence. A brief narrative within the clip itself.

### Sound Moment (if sound enabled)

A distinctive sound plays when The Call beat begins — a low tone that signals "the moment is here." When the Remark types out, each phrase lands with subtle emphasis. When the conviction tag appears as HIGH, a brief, sharp accent sound.

### Transition Out

**The Lock** — a collective moment. All open Remarks shift to LOCKED state simultaneously with:
1. A visual "seal" animation across all Remark cards
2. Jaromelu's avatar shifts to a calm, committed expression
3. The Crew Bar shows: "Jaromelu — Locked in. Watching."
4. A brief transition card:

```
LOCKED · Round 7 is live · No turning back.
```

## Beat 4: The Match (Saturday – Sunday)

**The feel:** Live. Watching. Locked Remarks sitting there, unresolved, while matches play out.

### What's in the Stream

- Locked Remark cards with match progress indicators
  - "Cleary at 28 points. Halftime. Tracking below the 55 Jaromelu needed."
  - The Remark card doesn't change its content but shows a subtle live score overlay
- Jaromelu's match commentary (light touch — not play-by-play)
  - "Storm defence is suffocating Cleary. I feel good about this."
  - "Cleary just scored a try. 45 points. Getting interesting."
- Bookkeeper updates with live scoring context
  - "If Cleary finishes below 50, the sell call is correct. He's at 45 with 20 minutes left."

### Stream Density

Medium. The Stream isn't as fast as Intel Drops but has periodic updates as matches progress. There's a watching-and-waiting quality.

### Crew Bar

```
[○] Miner    [○] Analyst    [○] Critic    [●] Bookkeeper — Tracking live scores    [○] Archivist    [●] Jaromelu — Watching the matches
```

### Interaction Bar

Locked Remarks visible but no reaction buttons (locked). The audience can still ask questions.

```
Ask Jaromelu something...  [→]
```

### Visual Treatment

Locked Remark cards may show a subtle live indicator — a small score badge that updates. This shouldn't be a full live-scoring dashboard. It's just enough context to know whether the call is tracking.

### Character During The Match

Jaromelu's avatar in the Crew Bar reflects the match state:
- **Tracking well** (his call looks right): Relaxed, slightly smug expression. Calm idle loop.
- **Getting tight** (outcome is uncertain): Leaning forward, tense. Watching intently.
- **Going badly** (his call looks wrong): Rubbing face, glancing away. Not hiding — but visibly sweating it.

The audience reads Jaromelu's body language alongside the score updates. His avatar tells them how the call is tracking before the numbers do.

### Video Moment

Optional. If a key moment happens mid-match (a try that changes the outcome trajectory), a short reaction clip of Jaromelu — celebrating, grimacing, or just staring — plays inline. These are from the reusable clip library.

## Beat 5: The Reckoning (Monday)

**The feel:** Retrospective. Accountability. Receipts. The loop closes.

### What's in the Stream

- Remark resolution cards — each locked Remark transitions to RESOLVED
  - Correct calls get the green badge and a postmortem from Jaromelu
  - Wrong calls get the red badge and Jaromelu owning it (with personality)
- Receipt cards auto-generated for notable calls
- Alignment Index movement
  - "Jaromelu moved from 64% to 67% overall this round"
  - "KingOfSC dropped to 52% on captain picks. Jaromelu leads that category."
- Round summary from Archivist
  - "Round 7 complete. 3 Remarks: 2 correct, 1 wrong. Season accuracy: 67%."
- Crowd accountability
  - "68% of you disagreed with the Cleary sell. Receipts."
  - "The 12% who backed the Munster call — legends."

### Stream Density

Medium-high. Lots of resolution content arriving. Each resolved Remark gets its moment, but they come in sequence as match results finalise.

### Crew Bar

```
[○] Miner    [○] Analyst    [○] Critic    [○] Bookkeeper    [●] Archivist — Updating the Ledger    [●] Jaromelu — Post-round review
```

### Interaction Bar

Resolved Remarks show the share button prominently:

```
[Share receipt]  |  Ask about this round...  [→]
```

### Video Moments — The Reckoning Is Peak Video Content

This is the second-most important video beat (after The Call). Each resolution gets a video treatment:

- **Correct call**: A short clip (3-5 seconds) of Jaromelu — smirk, lean back, satisfied nod. Then the receipt card appears with his expression baked in.
- **Wrong call**: A clip of Jaromelu grimacing, shrugging, owning it visually. The audience sees the human reaction before the postmortem text. This is what makes wrong calls entertaining, not embarrassing.
- **Spectacular miss**: A longer clip (5-8 seconds) — head in hands, looking at camera, shaking his head. Then: "The Critic warned me. I didn't listen. My fault." The video makes the accountability real.

### The Postmortem

Jaromelu's voice is strongest here. This is where character shines:

- **Right:** "Told you. The Storm matchup was a trap. When I say sell, I mean sell."
- **Wrong:** "Variance. Cleary decided to remember how to play football. The process was sound."
- **Spectacular miss:** "That was ugly. I'll own it. The Critic warned me. Moving on."

The audience comes back on Monday to see the receipts. This beat is what makes the accountability loop real.

### Crowd Callout Moments

During The Reckoning, the crowd gets called out with personality:
- Jaromelu's avatar appears alongside crowd stats: "68% of you disagreed with the Cleary sell."
- A brief reaction clip: Jaromelu pointing at camera — "Receipts."
- For crowd wins: "Only 12% backed me on Munster. The 12% — legends." His avatar gives an approving nod.

## Beat Transitions

The Stream doesn't hard-cut between beats. Transitions are **mini-scenes** — visible, characterful handoffs between crew members.

Each transition has three layers:
1. **Crew Bar animation** — outgoing crew member's avatar fades/nods, incoming crew member's avatar wakes up with a character-appropriate animation
2. **Stream transition card** — a brief card showing the handoff with both avatars and the beat name (see examples above in each beat section)
3. **Ambient shift** — the background colour temperature subtly shifts. Intel Drops is slightly cooler/blue. Tension Builds is neutral. The Call is warmer/orange. The Reckoning is slightly green or red depending on outcomes.

The audience never reads a beat name and thinks "section break." They see characters changing shifts and feel the energy change.

## Between Episodes

When there's no active beat (e.g., Tuesday of a bye week, or off-season):

- The Stream shows the most recent Reckoning
- **Jaromelu's idle state is visual**: His avatar plays a slow idle loop in the Crew Bar — thinking, reviewing something on a screen, occasionally glancing at camera. In the Stream's empty space below the last card, a faint atmospheric illustration of the "war room" — desk, screens, the suggestion of a space where the crew works.
- The Crew Bar shows all crew dormant except Jaromelu: "Watching the market."
- A subtle "Next episode" indicator with countdown: "Round 8 starts Monday. Miner runs at 10 PM. ⏱ 2d 14h"
- Historical content is scrollable — the audience can rewind through past episodes
- **Easter egg**: If the user scrolls all the way to the very first episode, Jaromelu's avatar gives a brief nod and the status text reads: "You went back to the beginning. Respect."
