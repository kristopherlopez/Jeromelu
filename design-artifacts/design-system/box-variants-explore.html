Box Variants

Eight different container styles for crew content. Same words, different material. Pick the ones that feel right — you can mix and match.

01

Left Border + Tint

The current approach. Functional but predictable — every AI design system uses this pattern. It's become a visual cliché.

02

Inline Mark

No container. Just a coloured mark/pip before the label, and the text flows naturally. Minimal, editorial, lets the content breathe. The "mark" could be a dot, dash, or small square.

03

Top Rule

A coloured horizontal rule above the content, like a magazine pull-quote or section divider. The colour sits above, not beside. Feels more editorial and less "component library."

04

Pull Quote

An oversized coloured quote mark or symbol as the visual anchor. The box type is communicated by the symbol itself — quote marks for takes, an asterisk for context, a warning triangle for warnings. Typographic, not structural.

05

Full Border

A thin coloured border all the way around. No background tint — the box is defined by its outline only. Cleaner, less heavy, the colour is in the frame not the fill.

06

Header Strip

A compact coloured header bar with the label, then neutral body below. The colour is concentrated at the top like a filing tab or a sticky note header. Body is just the default card surface.

07

Indented Block

No border, no background, no decoration. Just indented text with a coloured label. The surrounding whitespace IS the container. Maximum restraint — trusts the content and the typography to carry it. Very "Her" energy.

08

Bottom Rule

Content first, coloured rule underneath. The label is small and de-emphasised — the content leads. The rule acts as both a separator and a colour signal. Works well when boxes are stacked as a stream of notes.

Warning
Note

You don't have to pick one style for all box types. You could use Pull Quote for Jeromelu's takes, Top Rule for analyst context, Inline Mark for bookkeeper numbers, and Full Border for warnings. Mixing styles based on the role of the content can feel more intentional than one pattern for everything.
